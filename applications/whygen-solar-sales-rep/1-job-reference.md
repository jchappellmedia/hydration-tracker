# Job Description Reference — Solar Sales Representative, WhyGen Solar

## Application Details
- **Company:** WhyGen Solar
- **Role:** Solar Sales Representative
- **Location:** Tempe, AZ 85281 — In person (daily commute to office required)
- **Type:** Full-time
- **Pay:** $80,000 – $200,000+ per year (commission + bonuses), paid weekly
- **Benefits:** Flexible schedule, advancement opportunities, tuition reimbursement
- **Requirements:** Reliable transportation and personal vehicle, English, background check, no degree or experience required

## Plain-English Summary
WhyGen Solar hires commission-based sales reps to meet with homeowners, run consultations, present solar solutions, and close deals. Pay is commission plus bonuses with weekly payouts. They stress coaching, accountability, weekly performance standards, and a competitive culture. No experience or degree needed — they want drive, coachability, and consistency.

## What You'll Do
- Meet with homeowners and explain the benefits of solar.
- Run consultations and present customized solutions.
- Follow up, close deals, and get paid.
- Plug into ongoing coaching and leadership development.

## What They Want
- Competitive, coachable, and driven.
- Comfortable talking to people or ready to learn.
- Focused, consistent, results-oriented.
- Sales-minded, strong communicator, team player, quick learner.
- Reliable transportation; able to commute to the Tempe office daily.

## Application Questions (asked in the posting)
1. Are you open to being coached, held accountable, and meeting high performance standards every week?
2. Do you confirm you have no prior convictions or serious criminal offenses that would prevent meeting customers at their homes?
3. Are you willing to undergo a background check?
4. Do you have reliable transportation and can you commute to the office daily?
5. Do you have experience in roles where performance is measurable and results matter?
6. Have you received coaching or feedback and applied it to improve?
7. Do you have experience interacting with people, providing service, or persuading others?

## Culture Notes
Recruiting copy leans hard on hustle: "no excuses, no limits," leaderboards, trips, weekly standards. Expect a high-pressure, metrics-first sales floor.
